III. Culture

Diversity in Korea

Korea had been considered relatively homogeneous in its ethnicity and culture for a long time. However, Korea has experienced a greater and steady influx of foreign workers and immigrants since the 1990s, and the country is now becoming more and more ethnically diverse

When talking about someone’s ethnicity or nationality, you can use 사람 (native Korean word meaning person) or 인 (Sino-Korean word meaning person) after the name of the country. Notice that [nationality + 사람] has a space in between whereas [nationality + 인 ] does not.

American 미국 사람[Lit., America person] 미국인[Lit., America person]
