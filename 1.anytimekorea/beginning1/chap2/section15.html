3. ~은요?/~는요? “What about ~?; How about ~?”

meaning

~은요? literally means “as for ~?” but is best translated as “What about ~?” or “How about ~?”

샤오밍 씨는요?

Mr. Xiaoming as for

What about (you), Xiaoming?

Form

Use the rule below to choose between 은요? and 는요?

▶ Consonant-ending word + 은요?
마이클은요?
[ㄹ + 은요]
What about (you), Michael?
▶ Vowel-ending word + 는요?
마이클 씨는요?
[ㄹ + 는요]
What about (you), Michael?

Note 2

In Korean, the pronoun “you” is not frequently used. In place of “you,” use one’s name, job title (e.g., teacher 선생님, manager 매니저, section chief 부장님, etc), or family term (e.g., older brother 형/오빠, older sister 누나/언니, aunt 이모/고모, uncle 삼촌, etc.).

린다 씨는요?

What about (you), Linda?

선생님은요?

What about (you), Linda?

형은요?

What about (you), older brother?
