Note1

~쯤 is used at the end of a time or number expression and means “about; around; approximately.”

열다섯 명쯤 와요.

About fifteen people are coming.

한 시쯤 어때요?

What about around one o’clock?

Note2

~만 “only” shows the limitations of quantity or type of item.

다섯 명만 와요.

Only five people are coming.

A:

녹차 있어요?

Do you have green tea?

B:

아니요, 커피만 있어요.

No. I only have coffee.

Note3

~까지 means “up to; up until; to” It can be used with a time, number, or location word.

내일까지 할게요.

I will do it by tomorrow.

한 시까지 일해요.

I work until one o’clock.

도서관까지 같이 가요.

Let’s go to the library together.

Note4

~으로/~로 가다/오다

In Lesson 6, “~에 가요” was introduced, in which [place name] 에 means “to [place name].” Both “[place name] 에” and “[place name] 으로/로” mean “to [place name]” when they are used with the verbs “go” (가다) or “come” (오다). The slight difference between the two is that ~에 indicates the place as a destination, whereas ~으로/~로 focuses more on the direction one is headed.

▶ Consonant-ending word + 으로

:

집으로 가요.

▶ Vowel-ending word or ㄹ-ending word + 예요

:

학교로 가요;

지하철 subway로 가요.
