VI. Spontaneous Conversation

Now is a chance to have a realistic interaction with your classmates in Korean. You can practice speaking with your classmates, writing, and presenting in front of the class!

STEP 1.

Meet with three classmates and ask if they are club members, the name of their university, and their year in school. Please act out and create a meaningful conversation just as you practiced in the Guided Conversation.

Try to make your conversation flow naturally, following the given guidelines.

1. Decide which extra curricular activity group you want to belong to first (한국 드라마 클럽, 한국어 클럽, etc.) Then ask your classmates’ names and their membership status.

2. Ask which university they attend.

3. Ask what year of the university they are in.

4. Try to include -이/-가 아니에요; 저도 ~이에요/~예요; 정말요? in the conversation.

STEP 3.

Introduce your three classmates in front of the class. Alternatively, you may introduce your three best friends if you so choose for this writing practice.

While introducing them, you may point out to each person with an appropriate gesture and say his or her name. You may refer to each person as “이 사람은” or “이 친구는” which means “this person” or “this friend” respectively.

For example

이 사람은 (Classmate 1’s name)이에요/예요. 우리 (our) 클럽 멤버예요. 한국대학교 이 학년이에요. 그리고 ... Continue to introduce Classmate 2 and 3.
