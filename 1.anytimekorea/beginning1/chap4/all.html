3. Sentence ending : ~아요/~어요

meaning

You probably noticed that many of the sentences you've learned so far ended in ~요. ~요 is an honorific marker used to show respect to the listener. The ~요 ending can be dropped when you talk to a close friend or an acquaintance with equal or lower status. Compare the following two sentences.

(To your teacher)
지금 바빠요.
(I) am busy now

Usage

There are many verbs that have ~하다. Usually they have nouns (조깅, 공부, 쇼핑) in front of ~ 하다 seen in such compound verbs as 조깅하다 (derived from 조깅을 + 하다), 공부하다 (공부를 + 하다), 쇼핑하다 (쇼핑을 + 하다). VI. Spontaneous Conversation

Now is the chance to have a realistic interaction with your classmates in Korean. You can practice speaking with your classmates, writing, and presenting in front of the class!

STEP 1.

Invite three classmates and ask each of them to join what you want to do. Act out and create a meaningful conversation just as you practiced in the Guided Conversation!

Try to make your conversation flow naturally, following the given guidelines.

1. Ask what he or she is doing

2. Ask if he or she is busy at the moment.

3. Invite him or her to join your activities. (Refer to Ⅱ.1 Verbs and Adjectives.)

4. Try to include “지금; 혹시; 네; 같이; 음” in the conversation.

STEP 2.

Using ~아요/~어요 and the information you just collected from your classmates, write a narrative to describe each classmate.

For example,

이 사람은 (Classmate 1’s name)이에요/예요. Classmate 1 씨는 한국 드라마 봐요. 근데 (however), 안 바빠요. 그래서(therefore), 지금 같이 라면 먹어요. Continue to write about Classmate 2 and 3.

Present the short narrative of each classmate in front of the class. Try not to read what you wrote in Step 2.
