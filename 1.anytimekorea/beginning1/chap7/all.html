1. The subject-focus marker : -이/-가

Usage

Use -이-가to mark someone or something as the subject of a sentence.

1.

브라이언 Subject

이 학생이에요.

Brian Subject

is a student..
2.

에이미 Subject

가 바빠요.

Amy Subject

is busy.

When the subject is marked with -이/-가, the subject is focused as “new information” in the sentence.

1.
A:
누가 학생이에요?
Who is a student?
B:

브라이언 Subject

이 학생이에요.
Brian is a student.
[Interpretation: It is Brian who is a student.]
2.
A:
누가 바빠요?
Who is busy?
B:

에이미 Subject

가 바빠요.
Amy is busy.
[Interpretation: It is Amy who is busy.]
Form

Use the rule below to choose between -이 and 가

▶ Consonant-ending NOUN + -이

:

마이클이

[ㄴ + 이에요]

서울에 살아요.
Michael lives in Seoul.

▶ Vowel-ending NOUN + 예요

:

민수가

[ㅜ + 가]

뉴욕에 살아요.
Minsoo lives in New York.

Note1

There are some irregular variations when the subject is combined with the subject marker, -가. When the subject marker -가 is added to -저 (I) or 나 (I), they become 제 가 and 내가.

▶ 저 ‘I’ (humble form) + -가 → 제가

▶ 나 ‘I’ (plain form) + -가 → 내가

2. The topic/contrast marker : -은 or -는

Form

Use the rule below to choose between -은 and -는

▶ Consonant-ending word + -은:
민준은
[ㄴ + 은]
▶ Vowel-ending word + -는 :
에이미는
[ㅣ + 는]
Usage1

Use -은 and -는 to mark someone or something as the topic of a sentence. Topic is a piece of information (e.g., something, someone) that you want to talk about in your sentence and is already shared as “old information” between you and the listener in the conversation.

1.

민준 Subject

이 학생이에요.
Minjoon is a student.
[Interpretation: As for Minjoon, he is a student.]
2.

에이미 Subject

는 바빠요.
Amy is busy.
[Interpretation: As for Amy, she is busy.]
Usage2

When you have a person or a thing contrasted with another person or thing, mark the contrasted ones with -은 and -는

1.

저는 contrast

학생이에요.

에이미는 contrast

 학생이 아니에요.

[Interpretation: As for me, I am a student, but as for Amy, she is not.]

2.

마이클은 contrast

 파티에 가요.

린다는 contrast

파티에 안 가요.

[Interpretation: As for Michael, he goes to the party. (However,) as for Linda, she doesn’t go to the party.]

4. Verb base + ~을게요/~ㄹ게요 “I will ~”

Usage

This expression is used when you tell or promise the listener that you will do something.

Its meaning is equivalent to “I will do ~,” “I’ll volunteer to do ~,” “I promise that I will do ~.” Use ~을게요/ ~ㄹ게요 when you want to express your willingness in volunteering or promise for something. The subject of this expression is always “I” (or “we”). The action that the speaker says is often related or beneficial to the listener.

meaning

Its meaning is equivalent to “I will do ~,” “I’ll volunteer to do ~,” “I promise that I will do ~.” Use ~을게요/ ~ㄹ게요 when you want to express your willingness in volunteering or promise for something. The subject of this expression is always “I” (or “we”). The action that the speaker says is often related or beneficial to the listener.

Usage

Use the rule below to choose between ~을게요/ ~ㄹ게요

▶ verb base (ending in a consonant) + 을게요:
먹을게요.
[ㄱ + 을게요]
I will eat.
▶ verb base (ending in a vowel) + ㄹ게요:
갈게요?
[ㅏ + ㄹ게요]
I will go
* verb base (ending in ㄹ) + 게요:
만들게요?
[ㄹ + 게요]
I will make.
