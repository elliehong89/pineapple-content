II. Patterns, Expressions & Practice

1. ~으세요/~세요

Usage

In Lesson 4, you learned the ~ ~아요/~어요< ending (used to express respect for the listener). The new ending "~ 으세요/~세요" ” is a combination of the honorific marker ~ 으시/~” (used to express respect for the subject of the sentence) and the sentence ending ~어요. Koreans frequently use this ending to describe the actions or state of elders, such as 아버지(father), 어머니(mother), or 선생님(teacher). By using ~ 으세요/ ~세요, you can show your respect to the people who are older than you or who are higher in social status.The following examples show the usages of the ~아요/~어요 ending and the 으세요/~세요 ending. Note that the speaker uses ~아요/~어요 to describe Amy's actions or state of being, whereas the speaker uses ~으세요/~세요 to describe Professor Kim's actions or state of being.

1.
(talking about your friend, Amy)

에이미는 TV(를) 봐요.

Amy is watching TV.

(talking about your teacher)

김 선생님은 TV(를) 보세요.

Prof. Kim is watching TV.

2.
(asking your friend, Amy)

에이미 씨, 어디 가요?

Amy, where are you going?

(asking your teacher)

김 선생님, 어디 가세요?

Prof. Kim, where are you going?

3.
(asking your friend, Amy)

에이미 씨, 한국 영화 클럽 멤버예요?

Amy, are you a Korean movie club member?

(asking your teacher)

김 선생님, 한국 영화 클럽 멤버세요?

Prof. Kim, are you a Korean movie club member?

It's not that ~으세요/~세요 is used only to refer to elders' actions or state of being. It can be used to refer to the action/state of anybody other than you when you want to show courtesy to the person. You can elevate someone else by using ~으세요/~세요, but you shouldn't elevate your own self or your own actions.

1.
에이미 씨, 한국어(를) 공부해요? (O)

Amy, are you studying Korean?

에이미 씨, 한국어(를) 공부하세요? (O)

Amy, are you studying Korean?

2.
저도 한국어(를) 공부해요. (O)

I am also studying Korean.

저도 한국어(를) 공부하세요. (X)

I am also studying Korean.

2. 무슨 + NOUN “What ~; What kind of ~”

무슨 is always followed by a noun and means “what [NOUN]” or “what kind of [NOUN].” The difference between 뭐 (what) and 무슨 (what ~) is that 무슨 has to be followed by a noun.

뭐(를) 먹어요?
What are you eating?
무슨

음식 NOUN

are you eating?

4. ... 잘하시겠어요. “You must be good at ... .”

“... 잘하시겠어요” literally means “(You) must do ... well.” It means “You must be good at ... .”

1.
A:
저는 수영 선생님이에요.
I’m a swimming instructor.
B:
수영(을) 잘하시겠어요.
You must be good at swimming.
2.
A:
저는 셰프예요.
I am a chef.
B:
그래요? 요리(를) 잘하시겠어요.
Really? You must be good at cooking.
3.
A:
저는 프로 게이머예요.
I am a professional gamer.
B:
정말요? 게임(을) 잘하시겠어요.
Really? You must be good at gaming.
4.
A:
저는 보컬 트레이너예요.
I am a vocal trainer.
B:
그래요? 노래(를) 잘하시겠어요.
Really? You must be good at singing.
