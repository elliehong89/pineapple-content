VI. Spontaneous Conversation

Now is a chance to have a realistic interaction with your classmates in Korean. You can practice speaking with your classmates, writing, and presenting in front of the class!

STEP 1.

Meet three classmates one at a time and ask what their jobs are. If they are only students, you may ask their majors. Please act out and create a meaningful conversation just as you practiced in the Guided Conversation.

Try to make your conversation flow naturally, following the given guidelines.

1. Ask each other's job (or major) and make a positive comment

2. Try to include 우와~; 아~ 그래요?; 정말요?; 만나서 반갑습니다! in the conversation.

3. If you can, use some other expressions from previous lessons to create a more natural conversation.

STEP 3.

Introduce your three classmates in front of the class. While introducing them, say his or her name while pointing out to each person with an appropriate gesture. You may refer to each person as “이 사람은” which means “this person.” So far you learned various expressions in previous lessons 1~4. You should try extending your introduction using various expressions that you know in your writing.

For example,

이 사람은 (Classmate 1’s name) 이에요/예요. 직업은/전공은 (the name of his or her job / major) 이에요/예요. 그리고 ... Continue to introduce Classmate 2 and 3.

You may write your presentation script below, but try not to read it when presenting!
