A syllable is a unit of pronunciation that has one vowel sound combined with or without consonant(s). Because a syllable is a unit of pronunciation, a vowel and a consonant within the same syllable are pronounced together as a unit. For example, pronounce 가 (consonant ㄱ + vowel ㅏ) together as a unit. ㄱ and ㅏ are not pronounced separately.
