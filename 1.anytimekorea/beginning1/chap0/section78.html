7. Expressions used in classroom settings

The following is a list of classroom expressions your teacher or you may use in class. Listen to the audio and repeat after each sentence.
101

Korean
English translation
1.
잘 들으세요.
Please listen carefully.
잘 따라하세요.
Please repeat after me.
2.
해 보세요.
Please try it./Give it a try.
다시 해 보세요.
Please try it again.
다 같이 해 보세요.
Please try it all together.
3.
읽어 보세요.
Please read it.
4.
잘 했어요.

Well done; You did very well.
5.
A:

질문 있어요?

질문 없어요?

Do you have any questions?

Do you have any questions?

B:

네, 있어요.

네, 없어요.

Yes, (I) do.

Yes, (I) don’t.

6.

숙제 내세요.

숙제 주세요.

Please turn in your homework.

Please give me your homework.

7.
A:

아시겠어요?

Do you understand?

B:

네. or 예.

Yes.

A:

네, 좋아요.

Okay, good.

8.
A:

영어로 해도 돼요?

Can I speak in English?/Can I use English?

B:

한국말로 하세요.

Please speak in Korean.

9.
A:

Library, 한국말로 뭐라고 해요?

How do you say “library” in Korean?

B:

도서관이라고 해요.

It’s “library.” [Lit., It is said as library.]

10.
A:

오늘은 여기까지 해요.

Let’s wrap it up for today./Let’s call it a day.

B:

수고하셨습니다.

Thank you for your hard work (for your lecture)./
I really appreciate your help./You’ve done a good job

A:

내일 봐요.

See you tomorrow.
