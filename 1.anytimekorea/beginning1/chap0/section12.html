2.2 y-added vowels

When one additional short stroke is added to the simple vowels (ㅏ, ㅓ, ㅗ, ㅜ, ㅐ and ㅔ), they become ‘y sound’-added vowels (ㅑ,ㅕ, ㅛ, ㅠ, ㅒ, and ㅖ). The sound of each y-added vowel letter is similar to that of the bolded English letter(s) below.

ㅏ far

ㅓ up

ㅗ ohio

ㅡ book

ㅣ tree

ㅐ fan

ㅔ pen

simple vowels(8)

ㅑ (y + ㅏ) Yahoo

ㅕ (y + ㅗ) Yum

ㅛ (y + ㅜ) Yoga

ㅠ (y + ㅜ) You

ㅒ (y + ㅒ) Yam

ㅖ (y + ㅖ) Yes

y-added vowels(6)

Because ㅒ and ㅖ are mostly pronounced the same in modern Korean, you can only distinguish between ㅒ and ㅖ in their written forms.
