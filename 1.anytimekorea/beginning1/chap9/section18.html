3. 있으세요 and 계세요

In Lesson 6, two different meanings of 있다 were introduced: (1) to be; exist; live and (2) to have; possess. In this lesson, let’s focus on practicing the honorific forms of 있다/없다. To express the existence of someone to whom you show respect, use 계세요 and 안 계세요. When talking about someone’s possession that you want to show respect to, use 있으세요 and 없으세요.

-아요/~어요 form

-으세요/~세요 form

있다
(to be; exist; live)

있어요
계세요
(to have; possess)

있어요
있으세요

-아요/~어요 form

-으세요/~세요 form

없다
(to not be; not exist; not live)

없어요
안 계세요
(to not have; not possess)

없어요
없으세요

(1) existence: “계세요”/안 계세요

(asking your classmate)

지금 어디 있어요?

Where are you now?

(asking your boss)

지금 어디 계세요?

Where are you now?

(talking about your classmate)

마이클 씨 여기 없어요.

Michael is not here.

(talking about your teacher)

김 선생님 여기 안계세요.

Mr. Kim is not here.

(2) possession: “있으세요”/없으세요

(asking your classmate)

펜 있어요?

Do you have a pen?

(asking your boss)

펜 있으세요?

Do you have a pen?

(talking about your classmate)

펜 없어요?

You don’t have a pen?

(talking about your boss)

펜 없으세요?

You don’t have a pen?

Note1

Do not use the honorific forms (계세요/안 계세요/있으세요/없으세요) when you describe your own existence or possessions.

A:

지금 어디 계세요?

Where are you now?

B:

사무실에 있어요. (O)

I am in the office

사무실에 계세요. (X)

I am in the office

A:

펜 있으세요?

Do you have a pen?

B:

네, 있어요. (O)

Yes, I do.

네, 있으세요. (X)

Yes, I do.
