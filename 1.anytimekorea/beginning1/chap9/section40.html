It is common for Koreans to call each other using kinship terms even if they are not a “real” relative. For example, 이모 or 고모 is frequently used to call an owner of a small family-run soju bar where they sell drinks and dinner items. Some people call older an lady 어머니 and older gentleman 아버지 to be polite and friendly. When you hear someone referring to an old lady 어머니 on the street, it may be the case that the old lady is not the actual mother of the person!
