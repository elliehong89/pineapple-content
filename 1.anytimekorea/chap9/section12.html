2. 여기; 거기; 저기 here; there; over there

meaning

여기, 거기, and 저기 are place words meaning “here”, “there”, and “over there”, respectively.

[1] 여기 here

Use 여기 to point out person or thing that is closer to you.

남자:

리모콘 어디 있어요?

Where is the remote control?

여자:

여기 있어요.

(It) is here.

[2] 거기 there

Use 거기 to point out person or thing that is closer to the listener.

남자:

리모콘 어디 있어요?

Where is the remote control?

여자:

거기 있어요.

(It) is there (near you).

Note1

거기 can also be used when referring to a place that was already mentioned in the conversation. See the examples below.

(Talking about a Korean restaurant “Seoul Garden.”)

A: “서울 가든” 알아요?

Do you know Seoul Garden?

B: 네. 알아요.

Yes, I know.

A: 거기 어때요?

How is that place?

B: 거기 괜찮아요.

That place is alright.

(Talking about a Korean restaurant “Seoul Garden.”)

A: 에이미 씨, 집이 어디예요?

Amy, where is your hometown?

B: 시카고예요. 부모님이 거기 계세요.

It’s Chicago. My parents are there

[3] 저기 over there

Use 저기 to point out person or thing that is away from both the speaker and the listener.

남자:

리모콘 어디 있어요?

Where is the remote control?

여자:

저기 있어요.

(It) is over there.

Note1

“저기요” or “여기요” can also be used to say “Excuse me” as shown in the following examples.

1.

(Asking for directions)

A:

저기요

Excuse me.

여기 post office가 어디 있어요?

Where is the post office (near) here?

B:

저기 있어요.

It is over there

2.

(Asking for restaurant)

A:

저기요. (or 여기요.) 물 좀 주세요.

Excuse me. Give (me) some water, please.

B:

네.

Sure.

Note2

저기 or 저기요 is requently used as a hesitation word before requesting something from someone else. By saying 저기 (roughly meaning “um” in this context), you can draw the listener’s attention while also making your request less direct.

1.

A:

저기요…, 여기 우체국이 어디 있어요?

B:

우체국요? 저기 있어요.

A:

Um…, where is the post office (near) here?

B:

Post office? It’s over there.

2.

A:

저기요…, 남자 친구 있으세요?

B:

아뇨. 없어요. 왜요?

A:

Um…, do you have a boyfriend?

B:

No. I don’t. Why?

3.

A:

저기요…, 오늘이 무슨 요일이에요?

B:

토요일이에요.

A:

Um…, what day of the week is it today?

B:

It’s Saturday.
