II. Patterns, Expressions & Practice

1. ~잖아요. “you know, ~”

Usage

~잖아요 is used when you want to confirm something that you believe the listener is already aware of. Depending on the context, ~잖아요 can be translated as “you know~,” “Didn’t you know~?” or “Did you forget~?”

Form

Follow the rule below for when to use ~잖아요

▶ VERB or ADJECTIVE + 잖아요: 가잖아요; 좋잖아요.
▶ NOUN이다 + 잖아요
Consonant-ending NOUN + 이잖아요:
학생이잖아요.
[ㅇ + 잖아요]
Vowel-ending NOUN + 이잖아요:
한국어잖아요.
[ㅓ + 잖아요]
After a vowel-ending noun, 이 (as in 이다) is dropped, as in 한국어잖아요.
1.
오늘이
today
You know,
발렌타인데이잖아요.
Valentine's day is you know
it’s valentine's day today.
데이트 있어요?
date have
Do you have a date?
2.
다음 주가
next week
You know,
크리스마스잖아요.
Christmas is you know
it’s Christmas next week.
집에 가세요?
to home go
Are you going home?
3.
내일이
tomorrow
You know,
에이미 생일이잖아요.
Amy’s birthday is you know
it’s Amy’s birthday tomorrow.
깜짝 파티 어때요?
surprise party how about
How about a surprise party?
4.
A:
여기서 자주 쇼핑하세요?
Do you shop here often?
B:
네. 여기 싸잖아요 .
Yes. (Things are) cheap here, you know.
5.
에이미 씨, 언니 있잖아요. 언니도 대학생이에요?
Amy, you have an older sister, right? Is your older sister a university student, too?
6.
에이미 씨, 기숙사에 살잖아요. 기숙사 어때요?
Amy, you live in the dormitory, right? How is the dorm?
7.
A:
오늘 학교 안 가요?
Don’t you go to school today?
B:
오늘 토요일이잖아요.
It’s Saturday today, did you forget?
8.
에이미:
존 씨는 어떻게 한국말을 잘해요?
How does John speak Korean so well?
민준:
엄마가 한국 사람이잖아요.
His mom is Korean, didn’t you know?
에이미:
그래요?
Really?
9.
A:
마크 씨, 불고기 드실래요?
Mark, would you like some Bulgogi?
B:
저 고기 안 먹잖아요.
You know that I don’t eat meat.

Note1

있잖아요 is an idiomatic expression that is commonly used in colloquial speech. You might hear Koreans saying 있잖아요 at the beginning of the sentence, such as when they start the conversation or when they try to say something. Its meaning is similar to “You know what?,” “Look,” “Let me tell you,” or “I’ll tell you what.” It is used more frequently by women.

1.
에이미:
있잖아요. 마크 씨는 싱글이에요?
Do you know if... Mark is single?
민준:
네. 여자 친구 없어요.
2.
유미:
민준 씨, 있잖아요. 내일 에이미 생일이에요.
Minjoon, you know what? It’s Amy’s birthday tomorrow.
민준:
아, 그래요?
Oh, really?
