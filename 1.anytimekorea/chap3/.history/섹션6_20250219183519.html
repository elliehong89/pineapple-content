II. Patterns, Expressions & Practice

1. A은/는 B이/가 아니에요. “A am/are/is not B.”
meaning

In Lesson 2, you’ve learned “A은/는 B이에요/예요.” which means “Someone is NOUN” or “Something is NOUN.” The negative sentence of “A은/는 B이에요/예요.” “A은/는 B B 이/가 아니에요 which means “Someone/Something is not NOUN.”

(저는) 한국 사람이에요.. (positive sentence)

(I as for) Korean

(저는) 한국 사람이 아니에요.. (positive sentence)

Form

Use the rule below to choose between 이에요 and 예요.

▶ Consonant-ending NOUN + 이에요
: 김민준이에요.
[ㄴ + 이에요]
▶ Vowel-ending NOUN + 예요
: 에이미예요.
[ㅣ + 예요]

Remember your 한글 lesson. Consonants are ㄱ, ㄴ, ㄷ, ㄹ, ㅁ, ㅂ… and vowels are ㅏ, ㅓ, ㅗ, ㅜ, ㅡ, ㅣ…
Korean English
(Subject) NOUN 이에요/예요. (Subject) am/are/is NOUN.
김민준이에요.
김민준이에요.
에이미예요.

인턴이에요. 		(I) am Kim Minjoon.
(He) is Kim Minjoon.
(She) is Amy.
(It) is Hangeul.
(They) are interns.

Note 1
The subject often does not appear in a Korean sentence when it is understood among the speakers in the context.

Note 2
“NOUN이세요/ NOUN세요?” is the honorific form of NOUN이에요/ NOUN예요, respectively. You can use “NOUN이세요/ NOUN세요?” to show respect to the person who you are referring to. For more details, see Lesson 5. (Ⅱ.1)

You: 인턴이에요? Are you an intern?
You: 인턴이세요? Are you an intern? [Showing more respect]

Look at the following examples and see how ~예요 and ~이에요 are used.

  1. 김지호

    (Korean) 김지호예요.

    (English) (He) is Kim Jiho.

  2. 린다 존슨

    (Korean) 린다 존슨이에요.

    (English) (She) is Linda Johnson.

  3. 한국어

    (Korean) 한국어예요.

    (English) (It) is Korean.

  4. 바나나

    (Korean) 바나나예요.

    (English) (It) is banana.

  5. 이수빈

    (Korean) 이수빈이에요.

    (English) (I) am Yi Soobin.
