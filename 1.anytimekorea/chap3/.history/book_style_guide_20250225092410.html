Note style's

Note 1
The subject often does not appear in a Korean sentence when it is understood among the speakers in the context.

Note 2
“NOUN이세요/ NOUN세요?” is the honorific form of NOUN이에요/ NOUN예요, respectively. You can use “NOUN이세요/ NOUN세요?” to show respect to the person who you are referring to. For more details, see Lesson 5. (Ⅱ.1)

You: 인턴이에요? Are you an intern?
You: 인턴이세요? Are you an intern? [Showing more respect]

- The “base” of a verb or an adjective in the dictionary is the part that remains consistent.

- The “ending” of a verb or an adjective in the dictionary is the part that gets conjugated.

- You can conjugate a verb or an adjective for the following categories

1. Active/Passive form (먹어요/먹혀요)

2. Subject honorific formPassive form (먹어요/먹시죠)

3. Tense form (먹었어요, …)

4. Modal form (먹겠어요, …)

5. Sentence ending (먹어요, 먹습니다, 먹습니까, 먹자), 먹는다, ...)

In Beginning 1 & 2, you will learn active form, subject honorific (Lesson 5), tense form (Lesson 12), modal form (Lesson 5, 7, 8, 10, ...), and sentence ending (Lesson 4, 16). Let us study how to change the ~다 form to its ~아요/어요 form on the next page.
