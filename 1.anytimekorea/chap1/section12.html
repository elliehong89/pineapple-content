2. NOUN이요/요. “NOUN.”

Usage

Use “NOUN이요/요” to answer the question in the simplest way, mentioning only the very item in question. Compare the following two answers using either ~이에요/~예요 or ~이요/~요.

Question: Who is that person?
Answer 1:
김민준이에요.
(He) is Kim Minjoon
[Sentence-form answer]
Answer 2:
김민준이요.
Kim Minjoon.
[Simplest answer with NOUN]
Question:
성함이?
Your name (is)?
Answer 1:
에이미예요.
(I) am Amy.
[Sentence-form answer]
Answer 2:
에이미요.
Amy.
[Simplest answer with NOUN]
Form

Use the rule below to choose between 이요 and 요.

▶ Consonant-ending NOUN + 이요.

:

김민준이요.

[ㄴ + 이요]

▶ Vowel-ending NOUN + 요.

:

에이미요.

[ㅣ + 예요]

Note that Korean speakers of some dialects may use 요 in both cases.

Look at the following examples and see how ~이요/~요 is used to respond.

1.

A: 누구예요?

Who is this?

B: 지호요.

Jiho.

2.

A: 누구예요?

Who is this?

B: 마이클이요.

Michael.
