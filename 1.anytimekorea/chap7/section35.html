6. Expressing possession

에이미 핸드폰 means " Amy's cell phone." To express such relationship between the possessor (에이미) and the possessed (핸드폰) in Korean, put the possessor before the possession.

the possessor + the possession

에이미

친구

Amy's friend

친구

집

friend's house

선생님

책

teacher's book

제임스

커피

James's coffee

누구

커피

Whose coffee

의자

다리

the legs of a chair

교실

문

the door of a classroom

In addition, the following expressions for pronouns are also frequently used to show possession.

the possessor + the possession

제

거

mine (humble) [Lit.,my thing]

내

거

mine (plain) [Lit.,my thing]

누구

거

whose thing

Notice how expressions of possession are used in the conversations below.

1.
A:
핸드폰 번호가 어떻게 되세요?
What is your cell phone number?
B:
제 핸드폰요?
My cell phone?
A:
네.
Yes.

Note1

제 핸드폰 means "my cell phone." 제 (my) is a humble expression of 내 (my).

2.
A:
이거 누구 핸드폰이에요?
Whose cell phone is this?
B:
제 핸드폰이에요.
It is My cell phone.
(= 제 거예요.)
(= It is mine.)
3.
A:
제임스 씨, 이거 제임스 씨 핸드폰이에요?
James, is this your cell phone?
B:
네, 제 핸드폰이에요.
Yes. It is My cell phone.
(= 제 거예요.)
(= Yes. It is mine.)
