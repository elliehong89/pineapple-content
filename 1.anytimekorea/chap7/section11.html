2. The topic/contrast marker : -은 or -는

Form

Use the rule below to choose between -은 and -는

▶ Consonant-ending word + -은:
민준은
[ㄴ + 은]
▶ Vowel-ending word + -는 :
에이미는
[ㅣ + 는]
Usage1

Use -은 and -는 to mark someone or something as the topic of a sentence. Topic is a piece of information (e.g., something, someone) that you want to talk about in your sentence and is already shared as “old information” between you and the listener in the conversation.

1.

민준 Subject

이 학생이에요.
Minjoon is a student.
[Interpretation: As for Minjoon, he is a student.]
2.

에이미 Subject

는 바빠요.
Amy is busy.
[Interpretation: As for Amy, she is busy.]
Usage2

When you have a person or a thing contrasted with another person or thing, mark the contrasted ones with -은 and -는

1.

저는 contrast

학생이에요.

에이미는 contrast

 학생이 아니에요.

I am a student. (But) Amy is not

[Interpretation: As for me, I am a student, but as for Amy, she is not.]

2.

마이클은 contrast

 파티에 가요.

린다는 contrast

파티에 안 가요.

Michael goes to the party. Linda doesn't.

[Interpretation: As for Michael, he goes to the party. (However,) as for Linda, she doesn’t go to the party.]

3.
샤오밍 :

여기 냉장고 있어요?

Do you have a refrigerator here?

에이미 :

없어요.

I don’t.

샤오밍 :

에어컨은 contrast

 있어요?
(with 냉장고)

Do you have an air conditioner?

[Interpretation: As for the airconditioner, do you have it?]

에이미 :
네, 

에어컨은 contrast

 있어요.
(with 냉장고)

Yes, I do.

[Interpretation: As for the airconditioner, I have it.]

Note1

As a topic marker shown in Usage 1, -은 -는 can be dropped in colloquial and daily conversations. However, -은 -는 is not dropped in writing or formal speech.

(Writing/Formal speech)
에이미는 바빠요.
Amy is busy.
(Colloquial conversation)
에이미  바빠요.
Amy is busy.
(Writing/Formal speech)
브라이언은 학생이에요.
Brian is a student.
(Colloquial conversation)
브라이언  학생이에요.
Brian is a student.
(Writing/Formal speech)
이름은 뭐예요?
What is (your) name?
(Colloquial conversation)
이름  뭐예요?
What is (your) name?
(Writing/Formal speech)
제니는 가요.
Jenny goes.
(Colloquial conversation)
제니  가요.
Jenny goes.

Note2

As a contrast marker in Usage 2 -은/-는 is rarely dropped.

Note3

You cannot use -은 with -는 is rarely dropped.

에이미가는 바빠요.(x) → 에이미가 바빠요. OR 에이미는 바빠요. (o)

이름이름이는 뭐예요?.(x) → 이름이 뭐예요? OR 이름은 뭐예요? (o)
