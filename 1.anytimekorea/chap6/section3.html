Note

-하고 has two meanings “and” and “with,” depending where it is placed in the sentence.

(1)
하고 means “with” when it is placed after a noun (not between nouns):

NOUN 1하고 NOUN 2:

음식 NOUN1

하고

에이미 NOUN2

는 대학생이에요.

Xiaoming and Amy are college students.

(2)
하고 means “and” when it is placed between nouns:
저는

샤오밍 NOUN

하고 같이 공부해요.

I study together with Xiaoming.
