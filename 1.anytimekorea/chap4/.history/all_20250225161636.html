2. Conjugating verbs and adjectives

When making a sentence in Korean, you must change the dictionary ending (i.e., ~다 form) of the verb or adjective to its sentence ending (e.g., ~어요 form), as shown below.

(In the dictionary)

Base Ending

먹다

↓ conjugated

to eat

(In a sentence)

…………

먹어요.

(Amy) eats …

- The “base” of a verb or an adjective in the dictionary is the part that remains consistent.

- The “ending” of a verb or an adjective in the dictionary is the part that gets conjugated.

- You can conjugate a verb or an adjective for the following categories

1. Active/Passive form (먹어요/먹혀요)

2. Subject honorific formPassive form (먹어요/먹시죠)

3. Tense form (먹었어요, …)

4. Modal form (먹겠어요, …)

5. Sentence ending (먹어요, 먹습니다, 먹습니까, 먹자), 먹는다, ...)

In Beginning 1 & 2, you will learn active form, subject honorific (Lesson 5), tense form (Lesson 12), modal form (Lesson 5, 7, 8, 10, ...), and sentence ending (Lesson 4, 16). Let us study how to change the ~다 form to its ~아요/어요 form on the next page.

3. Sentence ending : ~아요/~어요
meaning

You probably noticed that many of the sentences you’ve learned so far ended in ~요. ~요 is an honorific marker used to show respect to the listener. The ~요 ending can be dropped when you talk to a close friend or an acquaintance with equal or lower status. Compare the following two sentences.

(To your teacher) 지금 바빠요.

(I) am busy now.

(To your teacher) 지금 바빠.

(I) am busy now.

Usage

When the 요 sentence ending is used with a verb or an adjective, it appears usually in the form of ~아요 or ~어요 ~아요 and ~어요 depends on the last vowel of the base form of a verb or adjective.

1. Regular verbs and adjectives

4. 안 + verb/adjective “not~”
Usage

You can make the sentence negative by placing 안 immediately before a verb or an adjective.

  • 1.

    저는 안 가요.

       

    I-as for not go

    I don’t go.

  • 2.

    커피 안 마셔요.

       

    coffee not drink

    (I) don’t drink coffee.

  • 3.

    안 좋아요.

       

    not be good

    (It) is not good.

  • 4.

    린다는 지금 안 바빠요.

       

    Linda-as for now not be busy

    Linda is not busy now.

There are many verbs that have ~하다. Usually they have nouns (조깅, 공부, 쇼핑) in front of ~ 하다 seen in such compound verbs as 조깅하다 (derived from 조깅을 + 하다), 공부하다 (공부를 + 하다), 쇼핑하다 (쇼핑을 + 하다). With these ~하다 compound verbs, the negative 안 is followed after the nouns. See the word order in the negative sentence with the ~하다 compound verbs in the following examples.

  • 1.

    저는 조깅(을) 안 해요.

       

    -as for jogging not do

    I don’t jog.

  • 2.

    지금 공부(를) 안 해요.

       

    now study not do

    (I) don’t study now.

  • 3.

    오늘 쇼핑(을) 안 해요.

       

    today shopping not be do

    (I) don’t shop today.

  • 4.

    오늘 숙제(를) 안 해요.

       

    today homework not do

    (I) don’t do homework today.

  • 5.

    저는 수영(을) 안 해요.

       

    -as for swimming not do

    (I) don’t swim.

  • 6.

    저는 노래(를) 안 해요.

       

    -as for song not do

    (I) don’t sing.

6. 같이 ~아요/어요. “Let’s ~.”

같이 means “together.” You can suggest a person to do something together using "같이 + ~아요/~어요."

  • 1.
    같이 먹어요
    together eat
    Let’s eat together.
  • 2.
    같이 가요
    together go
    Let’s go together.
  • 3.
    같이 공부해요
    together study
    Let’s study together.

You may respond to a suggestion by saying 네 and/or 좋아요 when you agree.

  • 1.
    A: 같이 가요
    B: 좋아요
    Let’s go together.
    Sure.
  • 2.
    같이 공부해요.
    B: 네, 좋아요.
    Let’s study together.
    Okay, That sounds good.
