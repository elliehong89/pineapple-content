II. Patterns, Expressions & Practice

1. Verbs and adjectives

Verbs are "actions" or "processes," whereas adjectives are "states" (i.e., size, weight, quality, quantity, shape, appearance, perception, and emotion).

Here are examples of frequently used Korean verbs. [Note: The (parenthesis) in the Korean sentences indicates that the object marker 을/를 (introduced in p.53) can be omitted often in casual conversation.]

Here are some frequently used adjectives in Korean. [Note: The (parenthesis) in the Korean sentences indicates that the marker 은/는 (to be introduced in detail in p.189) can be omitted often in casual conversation.]

Adjectives Korean English
좋아요 to be good 한국어(는) 좋아요. Korean is good.
바빠요 to be busy 지금 바빠요. (I) am busy now.
예뻐요 to be pretty 인형(은) 예뻐요. The doll is pretty.
싸요 to be cheap 가방(은) 싸요. The bag is cheap.
비싸요 to be expensive 가방(은) 비싸요. The bag is expensive.
많아요 to be many/much 학생(은) 많아요. There are many students.
[Lit., Students are many.]
커요 to be big 옷(은) 커요. The clothes are big.
배고파요 to be hungry
[Lit., stomach is hungry] 		배고파요. (I) am hungry.
배불러요 to be full
[Lit., stomach is full] 		배불러요. (I) am full.
매워요 to be spicy 음식(은) 매워요. The food is spicy.
맛있어요 to be tasty 불고기(는) 맛있어요. Bulgogi is tasty.
맛없어요 to be not tasty 라면(은) 맛없어요. Ramen is not tasty.

A Korean verb with "~아요/~어요form makes a statement. For example, "좋아요" can be translated into "is good" or "are good" and is placed at the end of a sentence.

1.

마이클은 바빠요.

Michael be busy.

Michael is busy.

2.

에이미는 예뻐요.

Amy be pretty.

Amy is pretty.
