4. 안 + verb/adjective "not~"

Usage

You can make the sentence negative by placing 안 immediately before a verb or an adjective.

1.

저는 안 가요.

I-as for not go

I don't go.

2.

커피 안 마셔요.

coffee not drink

I don't drink coffee.

3.

안 좋아요.

not be good

(It) is not good

4.

린다는 지금 안 바빠요.

Linda-as for now not be busy

Linda is not busy now.

There are many verbs that have ~하다. Usually they have nouns (조깅, 공부, 쇼핑) in front of ~ 하다 seen in such compound verbs as 조깅하다 (derived from 조깅을 + 하다), 공부하다 (공부를 + 하다), 쇼핑하다 (쇼핑을 + 하다). With these ~하다 compound verbs, the negative 안 is followed after the nouns. See the word order in the negative sentence with the ~하다 compound verbs in the following examples.

1.

저는 조깅(을) 안 해요.

I-as for jogging not do

I don't jog.

2.

지금 공부(를) 안 해요.

now study not do

(I) don’t study now

3.

오늘 쇼핑(을) 안 해요.

today shopping not do

(I) don’t shop today.

4.

오늘 숙제(를) 안 해요.

today homework not do

(I) don’t do homework today.

5.

저는 수영(을) 안 해요.

I-as for swimming not do

I don’t swim.

6.

저는 노래(를) 안 해요.

I-as for song not do

I don’t sing.

Note1

Please compare between positive sentences and negative sentences.

Positive sentences

Negative sentences

1. 저는 조깅해요. (○) 1. 저는 안 조깅해요. (X) → 저는 조깅(을) 안 해요. (○)
2. 저는 공부해요. (○) 2. 저는 안 공부해요. (X) → 저는 공부(를) 안 해요. (○)
3. 저는 쇼핑해요. (○) 3. 저는 안 쇼핑해요. (X) → 저는 쇼핑(을) 안 해요. (○)

Not all verbs and adjectives use 안 in order to make a negative sentence. A few verbs and adjectives have their own negative counterparts as shown below.

Positive form

Negative form

알다 (알아요) to know 모르다 (몰라요) to not know
있다 (있어요) to exist; to have 없다 (없어요) to not exist; to not have
재미있다 (재미있어요) to be fun 재미없다 (재미없어요) to be no fun

Note2

Please be careful not to say.

1. 저는 안 알아요. (X)

2. 저는 안 있어요. (X)

3. 저는 안 재미있어요. (X)
