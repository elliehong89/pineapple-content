4. Questions in Korean

In English, the subject-verb(S-V) word order switches when you change a statement sentence (S-V order) into a question (V-S order). However, in Korean the S-V order remains the same in both a statement and a question. To change a statement into a question, all you have to do is simply raise the intonation at the end of the sentence. (You also studied this in 5.6 Questions in Korean in Reading and writing Hangeul.)

If the question is a yes-no question, you can respond with either 네 (or 예) for "yes" or 아뇨 (or 아니요) for "no."

1.
A:
민준 씨, 인턴이에요?
Minjoon, are (you) an intern?
B:
네, 인턴이에요.
Yes, (I) am an intern.
2.
A:
마이클 씨, 미국 사람이에요?
Michael, are (you) American?
B:
아뇨,캐나다 사람이에요.
No, (I) am Canadian.
3.
A:
샤오밍 씨는 중국 사람이에요?
Is Xiaoming Chinese?
B:
네, 중국 사람이에요.
Yes, (He) is Chinese.
