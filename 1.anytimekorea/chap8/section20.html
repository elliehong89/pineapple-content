3. 그럼, ~ “If so ~ ; Well, then ~”

meaning

As we already studied in Lesson 6 (New Words & Expressions), -하고 has two meanings (i.e., “and” and “with”), depending where it is placed in the sentence. In this lesson, we’ll take a look at other usages and examples of -하고

1. -하고 means “and” when it is placed between nouns.

▶ NOUN 1 하고 NOUN 2

마크 NOUN 1

하고 

스티브 NOUN 2

는 룸메이트예요.

Mark and Steve are roommates.

▶ NOUN 1 하고 NOUN 2 하고 NOUN 3

1.

마크 NOUN 1

하고

스티브 NOUN 2

하고

제임스 NOUN 3

는 룸메이트예요.
Mark and Steve and James are roommates.
2.
A:

카페라테 하나 NOUN 1

하고

아메리카노 둘 NOUN 2

하고

카푸치노 하나 NOUN 3

주세요.
Please give me one Café Latte and two Americanos and one Cappuccino.
B:
네.
Okay.

2. -하고 means “with” when it is placed after a noun (not between nouns). In this case, -하고 is often used with 같이 “together,” such as -하고 같이 “together with.”

1.
오늘

마크 NOUN

하고 같이 공부해요.
(I) study together and Mark today.
2.
에이미는

제시카 NOUN

하고 같이 쇼핑해요.
(I) study together and Mark today.
3.
A:
마크 씨

저 NOUN

하고 같이 점심 먹을래요?
Mark, do you want to eat lunch together with me?
B:
네, 좋아요.
Sure. Sounds good.

Note1

In colloquial speech, -이랑/-랑 is frequently used instead of -하고 for the same meaning “and” and “with.”

Use the rule below to choose between the different forms.

▶ Consonant-ending NOUN + 이랑:
마이클이랑
[ ㄹ + 이랑 ]
▶ Vowel-ending NOUN + 랑:
친구랑
[ ㅜ + 랑 ]
아니에요.

마크랑 스티브는 룸메이트예요

Mark and Steve are roommates.

오늘 마이클이랑 같이 점심 먹어요.

(I) eat lunch together with Michael today.
