- The "base" of a verb or an adjective in the dictionary is the part that remains consistent.

- The "ending" of a verb or an adjective in the dictionary is the part that gets conjugated.

- You can conjugate a verb or an adjective for the following categories

1. Active/Passive form (먹어요/먹혀요)

2. Subject honorific formPassive form (먹어요/먹시죠)

3. Tense form (먹었어요, …)

4. Modal form (먹겠어요, …)

5. Sentence ending (먹어요, 먹습니다, 먹습니까, 먹자), 먹는다, ...)

In Beginning 1 & 2, you will learn active form, subject honorific (Lesson 5), tense form (Lesson 12), modal form (Lesson 5, 7, 8, 10, ...), and sentence ending (Lesson 4, 16). Let us study how to change the ~다 form to its ~아요/어요 form on the next page.

▶ 노트

1. 노트 스타일 1 (Normal)

Note1

The subject often does not appear in a Korean sentence when it is understood among the speakers in the context.

2. 노트 스타일 2

Note2

"NOUN이세요/ NOUN세요? is the honorific form of NOUN이에요/ NOUN예요, respectively. You can use "NOUN이세요/ NOUN세요? to show respect to the person who you are referring to. For more details, see Lesson 5. (Ⅱ.1)

You: 인턴이에요?

You: 인턴이세요?

Are you an intern?

Are you an intern? [Showing more respect]

3. 노트 스타일 3

Note3

You may begin with 아니요 or 아뇨 before "(A은/는) B이/가 아니에요."

Question:

브라이언 씨는 웨이터예요?

Is Brian a waiter?

Answer:

아니요 웨이터가 아니에요. 바텐더예요.

No, (he) is not a waiter. (He) is a bartender.

4. 노트 스타일 4

Note1

In B1, the speaker had better not repeat “파티에 안 가”(i.e., what has already been said in A’s question), which is more natural in the context. Thus B2 is preferred to B1.

Note2

As in B2, “바빠서요” is the combination of the adjective base 바쁘 (irregular “바쁘다”), the clausal connector ~아서 “because ~,” and the sentence ender ~요. For the irregular conjugation of 바쁘다, see Appendix V, #7.

▶ PATTERNS_EXPRESSIONS_PRACTICE

II. Patterns, Expressions & Practice

1. A은/는 B이/가 아니에요. "A am/are/is not B."

meaning

In Lesson 2, you've learned "A은/는 B이에요/예요." which means "Someone is NOUN" or "Something is NOUN." The negative sentence of "A은/는 B이에요/예요." is "A은/는 B 이/가 아니에요." which means "Someone/Something is not NOUN."

Usage

Use the rule below to choose between -이 아니에요 and -가 아니에요.
