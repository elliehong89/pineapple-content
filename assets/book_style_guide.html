Note1

In colloquial conversations, -이 -가 in NOUN 이/ 가 is often dropped.

멤버 아니에요.
인턴 아니에요.
한국 사람 아니에요.
커피 아니에요.
대학생 아니에요.

Note2

You may begin with 아니요 or 아뇨 before "(A은/는) B이/가 아니에요."

Question :

브라이언 씨는 웨이터예요?

Is Brian a waiter?
Answer :

아니요. 웨이터가 아니에요. 바텐더예요.

No, (he) is not a waiter. (He) is a bartender.

Note3

-하고 has two meanings "and" and "with," depending where it is placed in the sentence.

(1)
하고 means "with" when it is placed after a noun (not between nouns):

NOUN 1하고 NOUN 2:

음식 NOUN1

하고

에이미 NOUN2

는 대학생이에요.

Xiaoming and Amy are college students.

(2)
하고 means "and" when it is placed between nouns:
저는

샤오밍 NOUN

하고 같이 공부해요.

I study together with Xiaoming.

Note1

As a topic marker shown in Usage 1, -은 -는 can be dropped in colloquial and daily conversations. However, -은 -는 is not dropped in writing or formal speech.

(Writing/Formal speech)

에이미는 바빠요.

Amy is busy.

(Colloquial conversation)

에이미    바빠요.

Amy is busy.

(Writing/Formal speech)

브라이언은 학생이에요.

Brian is a student.

(Colloquial conversation)

브라이언    학생이에요.

Brian is a student.

(Writing/Formal speech)

이름는 뭐예요?

What is (your) name?

(Colloquial conversation)

이름    뭐예요?

What is (your) name?

(Writing/Formal speech)

제니는 가요.

Jenny goes.

(Colloquial conversation)

제니    가요.

Jenny goes.

Note1

"저기요" or "여기요" can also be used to say "Excuse me" as shown in the following examples.

1.

(Asking for directions)

A:

저기요

Excuse me.

여기 post office가 어디 있어요?

Where is the post office (near) here?

B:

저기 있어요.

It is over there

2.

(Asking for restaurant)

A:

저기요. (or 여기요.) 물 좀 주세요.

Excuse me. Give (me) some water, please.

B:

네.

Sure.

Note2

저기 or 저기요 is requently used as a hesitation word before requesting something from someone else. By saying 저기 (roughly meaning "um" in this context), you can draw the listener's attention while also making your request less direct.

1.

A:

저기요…, 여기 우체국이 어디 있어요?

B:

우체국요? 저기 있어요.

A:

Um…, do you have a boyfriend?

B:

No. I don't. Why?

1. A은/는 B이에요/예요. "A is B."

Usage

This is an extended explanation of "Ⅱ.1.NOUN 이에요/예요." in Lesson 1. When you introduce or identify someone or something, you can say [Someone or Something] A 은/는 [one's name or its name] B 이에요/예요. Thus, when you introduce or identify yourself, you can say 저는 A [your name] B 이에요/예요.

VI. Spontaneous Conversation

VI. Spontaneous Conversation

Now is a chance to have a realistic interaction with your classmates in Korean. You can practice speaking with your classmates, writing, and presenting in front of the class!

STEP 1.

Invite three classmates and ask each of them to join what you want to do. Act out and create a meaningful conversation just as you practiced in the Guided Conversation!

Try to make your conversation flow naturally, following the given guidelines.

1. Decide which extra curricular activity group you want to belong to first (한국 드라마 클럽, 한국어 클럽, etc.) Then ask your classmates' names and their membership status.

2. Ask which university they attend.

3. Ask what year of the university they are in.

4. Try to include -이/-가 아니에요; 저도 ~이에요/~예요; 정말요? in the conversation.

이 친구는 (Classmate 1's name)이에요/예요. 지금 기숙사에 살아요. 기숙사 방은 커요. 근데 조금 비싸요. 기숙사 방에 침대하고 책상하고 TV가 있어요. Expand your writing by adding more lines about the room description. Continue to write about other classmates as well.
